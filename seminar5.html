Personal Reflection

LOAD LIST
Speaker: ENGR. MANUEL ALONZO
That seminar by Engr. Archimedes Fresco really made me see Autodesk Revit and Building Information Modeling (BIM) in a whole new light. I knew a little bit about Revit before, but his talk really drilled down on how it brings all the different parts of a design together into one smart model. The fact that everyone working on a project can collaborate in real-time using this single model is pretty mind-blowing. What really stuck with me was how Revit can instantly update both the visuals and all the technical info. And it automatically generates schedules and layouts, which sounds like a huge time-saver and a way to seriously cut down on errors, especially for those massive projects. It hit me that if we want to be efficient and actually make a difference as engineers in the future, getting really good at BIM tools like Revit isn't just a plus—it's a must. That seminar totally got me excited to dive deeper into all these digital design platforms and see just how much they're changing the game in engineering.

CABLE SCHEDULE
Speaker: ENGR. MANUEL ALONZO
That cable scheduling seminar with Engr. Alonzo was a real eye-opener! I honestly hadn't given much thought to how important it is to properly document and organize cables in electrical engineering. I mean, tracking and labeling cables seemed kind of basic, but he showed how crucial it is for everything to run smoothly. The idea that giving each cable its own unique ID is such a simple thing but can save so much headache when you're making changes or trying to fix problems later on really clicked with me. And a cable schedule isn't just a list of wires; it's like a roadmap that tells you where each cable comes from, where it's going, what kind it is, and how it's routed. It's a seriously vital piece of the whole engineering puzzle. The part about cable routing was especially interesting. I didn't realize how much bad routing could mess things up – like causing overheating or interference that makes the whole system perform poorly. But the biggest takeaway for me was this shift in how I see cables. They're not just random wires; they're like the lifelines of the entire electrical system. Understanding that has made me appreciate how important precision and organization are in cable schedules. It's all about making sure the system is reliable, safe, and easy to maintain. Moving forward, I definitely plan on being way more disciplined about this in any electrical projects I work on.
